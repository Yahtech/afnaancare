What We Do

Caregivers play a crucial role in supporting individuals who need assistance with daily activities due to aging, illness, disability, or recovery from a medical procedure. Our services are tailored to meet the physical, emotional, and social needs of the care recipients, ensuring comfort, dignity, and independence.

Personal Care Services and Medication Management

At AfnaanCare, we assist with personal hygiene and grooming tasks to help care recipients maintain their health and dignity.
These include:

  • Bathing, dressing, and grooming.
  • Assistance with toileting and incontinence care.
  • Help with mobility, transferring, and positioning.
  • and support care recipients in taking their medications correctly and on time
  • Organizing and reminding about prescribed medications.
  • Monitoring for side effects or adverse reactions.
  • Coordinating with healthcare providers to ensure proper treatment.

Assistance with Daily Living Activities and Companionship and Emotional Support

We provide help with essential daily tasks that individuals may struggle to complete on their own and Providing emotional well-being and companionship is a vital aspect of caregiving

  • Preparing meals and ensuring proper nutrition.
  • Assisting with eating or feeding if required.
  • Help with mobility, transferring, and positioning.
  • Engaging in meaningful conversations and activities
  • Offering emotional support to combat loneliness and depression
  • Accompanying care recipients to appointments, social events, or errands

Health Monitoring and Specialized Care

We observe and report any changes in the care recipient’s condition to healthcare professionals or family members and provides a special care For individuals with specific health conditions, caregivers often provide tailored services

  • Monitoring vital signs like blood pressure, temperature, or blood sugar
  • Watching for signs of physical or mental decline
  • Acting as a liaison between the care recipient and healthcare providers
      • Alzheimer’s and Dementia Care
  • Managing behavioral challenges and providing memory-related support.
  • Creating a safe environment to prevent wandering or injuries
      • Post-Surgery Care
  • Assisting with wound care, mobility, and recovery exercises.
  • Ensuring adherence to medical instructions.
      • Disability Support
  • Adapting tasks and routines to accommodate physical or mental disabilities.
  • Encouraging independence where possible.

Transportation Services and Respite Care for Families

We often assist with or provide transportation to ensure the care recipient can:

  • Attend medical appointments.
  • Run errands such as grocery shopping.
  • Engage in social or recreational activities.

    • We also provide temporary relief to family members who are primary caregivers:

  • Taking over caregiving responsibilities for short periods.
  • Allowing family caregivers time to rest and recharge.
